Overview

This small web app is meant to keep track of embroidery jobs (school uniforms, business polos, jackets, hats, etc.). It does not replace your POS system, but it gives you a clearer view of what is on the embroidery machine and what is ready.

  • Dashboard: quick summary of job counts.
  • Add Job: form for adding new embroidery orders.
  • View Jobs: list of all jobs with filters.
  • Search: find a job quickly while on the phone with a customer.

Statuses

Each job should have one of these statuses:

  • Intake – Job was created. Garments are in the shop or on order.
  • Art / Digitizing – Artwork is being prepared or changed.
  • In Progress – Job is being framed/hooped/stitched.
  • Ready for Pickup – Finished and bagged, waiting for the customer.
  • Completed – Customer picked up or order was shipped and paid.

Important Fields

  • Customer Name: person you will contact.
  • School / Business: example: “St. Mark”, “Burke Electrical”.
  • Garment / Item Type: polo, hoodie, scrub top, hat, etc.
  • Size Range: “YS–AXL”, “10 assorted sizes”, etc.
  • Thread Colors: include real thread numbers if you have them.
  • Logo Notes: any notes about how to place or scale the design.
  • Promised Due Date: when you told the customer it would be ready.
  • PO / Invoice #: reference to your POS / invoicing system.
  • Internal Notes: anything extra for whoever runs the machine.

Common Questions

What if a job changes?

If quantities or due dates change, update the job in the database and add a short note so you remember what changed.

How do I find a job when someone calls?

Go to the Search page and type their name, school, or PO number. You can also filter by status or due date if needed.

Who should update the status?

Usually, the person doing the work at that step (intake, art, embroidery, front counter) should switch the status when they move the job along.
